Classical Music Appreciation For Beginners

I studied piano from age 6-15. Although I haven't had much time to play since, I remain an aficionado of classical music. By classical music I mean "precise music", which includes music from the Baroque era, the classical era, the romantic era, the impressionist era, etc. Do you aspire to be a connoisseur of classical music but get bored or get lost easily? It's easier to appreciate a piece if you know the theme/story/picture connected to it. Here are some examples.

Beethoven: Piano Sonata No.17 in D Minor "Tempest"

Brahms: Hungarian Dances

Debussy: "La Neige Danse (The Snow Dance)" from Children's Corner

Chopin: Piano Sonata No.2 in B-Flat Minor, 3rd Movement "Marche Funèbre"; Polonaise in A Major, Op.40 No.1 "Military"; Polonaise in A-Flat Major, Op.53 "Heroic"; Étude in C Minor, Op.10 No.12 "Revolutionary"; Étude in A-Flat Major, Op.25 No.1 "Aeolian Harp"; Étude in A minor, Op.25 No.11 "Winter Wind"; Waltz In D-Flat Major, Op.64 No.1 "Petit Chien (Smol Pupper)"

Ravel: La Mer (The Sea), L.109; Jeux d'eau (Playing Water)

Saint-Saën: Carnival of The Animals; Danse Macabre, Op.40

Mozart: "Dies Irae" from Requiem in D Minor, K.626

Liszt: La Campenella (The Clock); La Lugubre Gondola (The Lugubrious Gondola), S.200, No.1; "Erlkönig" from 12 Lieder von Franz Schubert, S.558

Mendelssohn: Lieder ohne Worte, Op.19 No.3, Molto allegro e vivace "Jagerlied (Hunting Song)"

Mahler: Kindertotenlieder

Scriabin: Étude in D-Sharp Minor, Op8 No.12 "Pathetique"
