I am a PhD candidate in Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering at University of California, San Diego (UCSD). I received my B.S. degree at University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) in 2013 and my M.S. degree at University of California, San Diego in 2015.
My current research area is stochastic control theory and its application to physics (e.g. Schrödinger equation). My further research interests include, but not limited to, application of (stationary action) control theory in conservative dynamical systems, application of Malliavin calculus in stochastic control theory.
My Erdős Number is 4. (R. Zhao - W. McEneaney - W. Fleming - R. Bellman - P. Erdős)

Solving The Schrödinger Equation Using Stochastic Control Method
A point mass rotates around a charged nucleus (assumed to be very far from the point mass) under electrostatic force. An approximation of the wave function as a series expansion is obtained through the use of stationary propagation of action functional and a complex-valued diffusion representation. The Probability density functions associated with the quadratic and cubic terms from the series are shown in the figure; the distribution of a free partical in radial direction and in plane are recovered.
