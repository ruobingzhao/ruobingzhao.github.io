Ruobing Zhao

I am a PhD candidate in Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering at University of California, San Diego. I received my B.S. degree at University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) in 2013 and my M.S. degree at University of California, San Diego in 2015.
My current research area is stochastic control theory and its application to physics (e.g. Schrödinger equation). Here is a demonstration of some results:
A point mass rotates around a charged nucleus under electrostatic force. An approximation of the wave function as a series expansion is obtained through the use of stationary action functional and a complex-valued diffusion representation. The Probability density functions associated with the quadratic and cubic terms from the series are shown in the figure.
My further research interests include application of stochastic control in biology (e.g. chemical reation networks, tumor growth) and finance (e.g. continuous-time portfolio selection).
