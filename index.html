Welcome

Ruobing Zhao

I am a PhD candidate in Mechanical & Aerospace Engineering at University of California, San Diego. I received my B.S. degree at University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA) in 2013 with a major in Chemical Engineering and a minor in Mathematics, and I received my M.S. degree at University of California, San Diego in 2015.
My current research area is stochastic control theory and its application to physics (e.g. Schrödinger equation). I am also interested in application of stochastic processes in biology (e.g. chemical reation networks, tumor growth.) and finance (e.g. continuous-time portfolio selection).
A point mass rotates around a charged nucleus under electrostatic force. An approximation of the wave function as a series expansion is obtained through the use of stationary action functional and a complex-valued diffusion representation.
